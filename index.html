About me
Rabbi Sydney Mintz has served as Rabbi of Congregation Emanu-El in San Francisco for the past 24 years. She/They was ordained at the Hebrew Union College-Jewish Institute of Religion in New York City in 1997 where she will be awarded her honorary Doctorate of Divinity in 2022 . Mintz was a Senior Rabbinic Fellow at the Shalom Hartman Institute in Jerusalem in 2004.

As a Performer/Writer, Rabbi Mintz’s one woman show “You May Think I’m Funny, But It’s Not” recently premiered and sold out at the Marsh Theatre in San Francisco. The show paused during Covid and is now getting ready to hit the road again.

In her role on the advisory board of The Reimagine End of Life Festival, Rabbi Mintz uses humor to examine how we die, grieve and bury our dead through an environmental and psychedelic lens. She performed at the Norse Theatre along with author Annie Lamott. Rabbi Mintz is considering burying her own body in a magic mushroom suit.

As a spiritual leader and activist Rabbi Mintz has served on the national Board of Bend the Arc: A Jewish Partnership for Justice, Rabbinic scholar for American Jewish World Service and is the founder of the award winning Late Shabbat Young Adult Program at Congregation Emanu-El. Musical and uplifting, this creative and innovative program at Temple Emanu-El has served thousands of young adults as they rediscover and redefine their Judaism on their own terms.

Rabbi Mintz is the co-creator of the Book of Life Project with Rabbi Matthew Gewirtz and Rabbi Yoshi Zweiback, which connects people internationally with ancient and cutting edge Jewish wisdom to enable us to write our own Books of Life. These topics and many more have been explored in conversation with Dr. Larry Brilliant, Authors Anne Lamott and Noa Tishby, Producers and Journalists Juju Chang and Neal Shapiro. Rabbi Mintz has also interviewed Dr. Richard Miller, author of Psychedelic Medicine, author and journalist Reza Aslan and author Bari Weiss.

She/They is currently working on projects in the areas of jewish spirituality, psychedelics and death, performance, theatre and queer culture. You can reach her/them @Rebsyd and sydmintz@gmail.com
